What is a Personal Loan?

A personal loan is an unsecured loan offered by banks and NBFCs to individuals to meet their financial requirements. The principal loan amount, along with the interest, is repaid by borrowers in equated monthly installments (EMIs) over a pre-decided tenure, usually ranging from 1 to 5 years. To qualify, applicants must meet certain eligibility criteria based on their credit profile, income, repayment capacity, etc.

Why Choose a Personal Loan?

Borrowers often choose a personal loan to meet their planned or immediate financial requirements owing to its fast approval, usage flexibility, and minimal documentation. Here are a few advantages that make personal loans a preferred borrowing option.

No Collateral Required

As personal loans are unsecured in nature, a borrower does not need to pledge property, gold, or other assets.

Flexible End Use

Personal loans can be used for personal expenses, medical emergencies, travel, weddings, education, debt consolidation, etc., as long as they are not for illegal or speculative purposes.

Quick Approval and Disbursal

The hassle-free and fully online process often enables instant loan approval and faster disbursal depending on the eligibility of applicants.

Simple Documentation

Digital processes have made documentation relatively easier, with approval now primarily based on income, credit score, repayment capacity, etc.

Fixed EMIs and Tenure Clarity

As personal loan interest rates are fixed, EMIs and tenure also don’t change during the loan period helping one plan repayments efficiently, which helps in better financial planning.

Improves Credit Mix

Adding an unsecured product and repaying it diligently improves your credit portfolio that positively impacts your creditworthiness over time.

5 Tips to Choose the Best Personal Loan Online

Choosing the best personal loan requires careful consideration. At Paisabazaar, we help you compare the best personal loan offers from top lenders with attractive interest rates and flexible repayment plans. Here’s how you can choose the best personal loan for you:

1. Compare offers

Check each offer’s rate range, tentative EMI, maximum loan amount, expected disbursal time, and mandatory documents. Shortlist 2 to 3 offers that balance cost and speed.

2. Check offers from banks/NBFCs you already bank with

Existing relationships (salary/savings account, FD, credit card) can mean pre-approved limits, quicker KYC, and sometimes better rates or lower processing fees. It is advisable to check with these lenders and see if you can avail better offers.

3. Compare the total cost of credit, not just the interest rate

Confirm processing fees, foreclosure lock-in, part-prepayment rules, and all penalties: late payment, cheque/mandate bounce, ECS return, statement charges, etc. Be thorough to avoid any mishap later.

4. Estimate affordability

Use the EMI calculator on Paisabazaar to select different tenures and interest rates. While selecting, choose the shortest tenure you can comfortably afford to cut interest outgo, but leave room for emergencies.

5. Choose what fits your requirements and repayment capacity

Pick the shortest tenure you can comfortably afford to cut interest outgo, but leave room for emergencies. Prefer lenders that allow free/low-cost part-prepayments so you can reduce tenure later.

Personal Loan Eligibility Criteria

Personal loan eligibility criteria may vary across lenders. To give you a fair idea, here are some of the general requirements:

  • ✓ Credit Score: Preferably 750 and above
  • ✓ Salary: Usually Rs. 15,000–30,000 a month (Do note: if you have an existing relationship with the bank through a savings account, income eligibility may be lower)
  • ✓ Income: Usually Rs. 3 lakh p.a. (may vary among self-employed professionals & non-professionals)
  • ✓ Employment Stability: At least 1 to 2 years of total work experience with a stable current job
  • ✓ Employer Profile: Salaried employees working with the government, Public Sector Undertakings, MNCs and other reputed corporates are favoured.
  • ✓ Age Limit: Usually, 21–60 years. For self-employed applicants and pensioners, the maximum age limit may go up to 65 and 75 years, respectively.

    • Personal Loan VS Business Loan- What should You Choose?

    • Knowing whether you need a personal loan or a business loan can help you get the loan that suits your requirements the best. Let us understand the difference between both loan types so that you can make an informed decision when required.

    • Parameter Personal Loan Business Loan
      Purpose For personal expenses such as travel, medical, wedding, education, etc. For business needs such as working capital, expansion, inventory, salaries, cash flow, etc.
      Eligibility Basis Mainly based on individual income, credit score, employment stability. Based on business turnover, profits, cash flow, GST returns, ITR, credit score, and business vintage.
      Documentation Minimal: KYC, income proof, bank statement, salary slips/ITR. Extensive: KYC, business registration, GST returns, ITR, financial statements, bank statements, etc.
      Loan Amount Typically lower: Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 40 lakh Higher eligibility: Rs. 1 lakh to Rs. 2 crore (or more based on lender).
      Interest Rates Usually fixed; higher than secured loans. Varies based on business risk; may be higher or lower than personal loans depending on profile.
      Collateral Requirement No collateral needed for unsecured personal loan. Mostly unsecured but can be secured for larger loan amounts.
      Tenure 1 to 6 years. 1 to 10 years; working capital loans may have flexible/renewable tenure.
      Processing Time Faster approvals with some loans disbursed on the same day. Longer evaluation due to business assessment; 2–7 days typically.
      Usage Flexibility Very flexible, can be used for any personal purpose. Must be used strictly for business-related activities.
      Tax Benefits No tax benefits on interest. Interest and processing charges can be claimed as business expenses, reducing taxable income.
      Ideal for Salaried or self-employed individuals needing quick personal funds. Entrepreneurs, MSMEs, self-employed professionals needing capital for business growth.

      FAQs

      What is the interest rate on a personal loan? +
      Personal loan interest rates offered by leading banks and NBFCs start from 9.98% p.a. depending on the credit profile of their loan applicants. However, some public sector banks may offer lower interest rates to their personal loan applicants
      How much CIBIL score is required for a personal loan? +
      Applicants with a credit score of 750 and above usually have higher chances of availing personal loans. However, many banks and NBFCs offer loans to individuals with low CIBIL score/credit score but charge higher interest rates to compensate for the higher credit risk associated with those lenders.
      Can I take personal loans from multiple banks at the same time? +
      Yes, you may avail personal loans from multiple lenders at the same time, provided the lender of your second personal loan is satisfied with your repayment capacity. However, it is better to take a single loan of a larger amount from the lender providing lower interest rates and/or better terms and conditions.
      How much time does it take to disburse a personal loan? +
      Usually, it takes anywhere from a few hours to a few business days to disburse a personal loan. However, it depends on the lender’s policies, processes, and related timelines. However, if you are a pre-approved customer of a bank or NBFC then you can get instant approval with quick disbursals.
      Can I top up my personal loan? +
      Most lenders offer top-up personal loans to existing borrowers with a good repayment track record. The eligibility depends on factors like repayment history, income, and the lender’s policies.
      Is a personal loan taxable? +
      No, personal loan or any kind of loan is not treated as your income, and is not taxable.
      What happens if I miss a personal loan repayment? +
      Missing a repayment can lead to late payment charges and a negative impact on your credit score. If delays continue, the lender may report it to credit bureaus, making it harder for you to access loans in the future. In extreme cases, legal action or collection efforts may follow.
      Can I get a personal loan with bad credit? +
      While it might be difficult, it is not impossible. Apart from your credit score, there are other factors that are taken into account by lenders to assess your creditworthiness. These include your income, employment stability, etc. Keeping them intact might increase your chances of getting a personal loan.
      Can I prepay my personal loan? Does it impact credit score? +
      Yes, you can prepay your loan. However, lenders usually have a lock-in period for loans along with prepayment charges. Check with your bank before prepaying.Your credit score might be affected positively by prepayment.
      Will my credit score be impacted when I apply for a personal loan? +
      When you apply for a personal loan, your credit score might go down a bit for initial months. Maintain good credit behaviour to improve your credit score.
      How does personal loan work? +
      A personal loan is a credit facility that banks and NBFCs offer to eligible applicants to meet their financial needs. An interest is charged on the loan. The loan amount, along with the interest, is repaid in the form of EMI for a pre-determined period.
      What is the highest loan amount I can get? +
      The maximum loan amount depends on your income, credit score, employer profile, and lender policies. Typically, it can range from a few thousand rupees up to ₹40 lakh or more.
      How can I repay my personal loan? +
      You can repay through fixed monthly EMIs via ECS, NACH, or post-dated cheques. Some lenders also allow prepayment or foreclosure, subject to their terms.
      Do I need to provide any security or collateral to apply for a personal loan? +
      Mostly, all personal loans are unsecured. You do not need to provide any form of security or collateral for an unsecured personal loan.
      Can I get a personal loan if I am self-employed? +
      Yes, most lenders offer personal loans to self-employed individuals. However, the eligibility criteria for self-employed can be slightly different as compared to personal loans for salaried employees.
      Why was my personal loan application rejected? +
      There can be multiple reasons for personal loan rejection. A few include low credit score, poor credit history, inconsistent income, high debt-to-income ratio and more.
      Can I apply for a personal loan jointly with my spouse? +
      Yes, you can jointly apply for a personal loan with your spouse. In fact, applying jointly may increase your chances of loan approval and a higher loan amount.
      How to foreclose a personal loan? +
      To foreclose your loan, pay the entire outstanding balance before the loan tenure ends. However, check with your lender for the foreclosure charges and the lock-in period.
      How to settle a personal loan? +
      To settle your loan, approach your lender, explain genuine financial hardship with proof, and request a one-time settlement after missed repayments. The bank may agree to close the loan at a reduced lump sum amount, which must be documented in writing. Pay off the negotiated amount. Once paid, the account is marked “settled” in your credit report, which can impact your future loan eligibility.
      What factors determine my eligiblitiy for a personal loan? +
      Many factors, including your age, income, credit score, repayment history, debt-to-income ration, etc determine your eligibility for a personal loan.
      Is it safe to take a personal loan online? +
      Yes, most lenders offer online facilities to individuals to apply online. Online marketplaces like Paisabazaar also offer the convenience of applying for loans online.
