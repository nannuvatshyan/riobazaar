Factors Affecting Personal Loan Interest Rates

Banks and NBFCs primarily set personal loan interest rates based on the cost of funds and the credit risk evaluation of their loan applicants. Here are the factors affecting personal loan interest rates:

  • ✓ Credit Score: Lenders usually assess the credit scores of their applicants as an initial check of their creditworthiness. Applicants with credit scores of 750 or above are often viewed as financially disciplined and, thereby, have lower chances of loan defaults. Many lenders offer personal loans at lower interest rates to such loan applicants.
    • ✓ Income: Some lenders may also offer preferential interest rates to personal loan applicants having higher monthly income as loan applicants with higher incomes may reduce credit risk for the lenders.
      • ✓ Occupation Profile: Lenders usually offer preferential interest rates to salaried individuals as compared to self-employed applicants due to the former’s higher income security. Within salaried applicants, government and PSU employees are offered lower interest rates than private sector employees due to the former’s higher job security.
      • ✓ Existing lending or banking relationship with the lender: Many lenders offer interest rate concessions or pre-approved instant personal loans at lower interest rates to applicants with whom they have existing lending, deposit, or credit card relationships.
        • ✓ Employment History: Lenders assess the employment stability of an applicant when setting interest rates. Applicants who frequently change jobs are often considered to carry higher credit risks, which can lower their chances of approval or result in higher interest rates.

Types of Personal Loan Interest Rates

When applying for a personal loan, one of the key decisions you’ll need to make is whether to choose a fixed or floating interest rate. Understanding each type of interest rate can help you make an informed decision based on your financial goals. Here is an overview of both options:

Fixed Interest Rates

In case of fixed interest rate personal loans, as the term implies, the interest rate remains the same for the entire loan tenure. As a result, your EMIs remain constant and therefore easier for borrowers to plan their budget. However, fixed rate loans usually come with a higher spread than floating rates.

Floating Interest Rates

In case of personal loans availed at floating interest rates, the interest rate fluctuates over time based on changes in the linked benchmark rate (repo rate). Note that the EMIs would increase only in the case of floating rate personal loans availed with the ‘EMI change’ option. In this case, the tenure would remain the same irrespective of the changes in the interest rates. In case of floating rate personal loans availed with ‘tenure change’ option, the EMI would remain the same while the tenure would change as per the changes in the interest rates.

How to Get a Personal Loan with Low Interest Rate?

The interest rates of personal loans can significantly impact your overall borrowing costs. Here are five tips you can follow to improve your chances of availing a personal loan at a lower interest rate:

  • ✓ Maintain a good credit score of 750 or above – by timely repayment of credit card bills and/or EMIs, avoid frequent multiple credit card applications or loans, etc.
  • ✓ Consider checking with the lender(s) where you hold a deposit/loan account/credit card. These lenders usually offer loans at preferential interest rates or extend pre-approved offers.
  • ✓ Maintain job stability and avoid frequent employment changes, as it would reflect income instability.
  • ✓ Visit Paisabazaar to compare personal loan offers from multiple lenders based on their interest rates, loan tenure, amounts, disbursal times, fees and other charges.
  • ✓ Avoid making multiple personal loan applications within a short duration as it would reduce your credit scores, thus making it harder to get your personal loan approved at lower interest rates.

How Interest Rates Affect Your Loan Tenure and EMIs?

Personal loan tenures usually range from 1 to 5 years with some banks and NBFCs offering longer tenures of 6, 7 or 8 years. The choice of loan tenure directly affects the borrower’s EMIs and total interest cost. Opting for a longer tenure lowers loan EMIs, making repayment easier for borrowers having limited repayment capacity. However, this convenience comes at the cost of incurring higher interest costs. Borrowers who choose longer tenures can reduce their total interest cost by making prepayments, whenever they have surplus funds. Personal loan applicants should opt for shorter tenures only when they have adequate repayment capacity for the EMIs.

Let’s Take an Example: Rs 5 Lakh Loan EMI Across Tenures

Below is the EMI, total interest cost and total amount payable for different tenures of a personal loan of Rs 5 lakh at an interest rate of 11.50% p.a.

Tenure Interest Rate (p.a.) EMI Total Interest Total Payable
1 year 11.50% Rs 44,308 Rs 31,690 Rs 5,31,690
2 years 11.50% Rs 23,420 Rs 62,084 Rs 5,62,084
3 years 11.50% Rs 16,488 Rs 93,568 Rs 5,93,568
4 years 11.50% Rs 13,045 Rs 1,26,136 Rs 6,26,136
5 years 11.50% Rs 10,996 Rs 1,59,778 Rs 6,59,778



  • Analysis:

    • Choosing a longer tenure lowers your EMI, making monthly repayments more manageable. For instance, EMI reduces from Rs 44,308 (1-year tenure) to Rs 10,996 (5-year tenure). But, longer tenure comes at a cost as it significantly increases the total interest cost over the repayment period. For example, with a 5-year loan, you pay Rs 1.59 lakh in interest, which is over 5 times more than the Rs 31,690 paid on a 1-year loan

    Key Takeaway

    If you have adequate repayment capacity, you can opt for shorter tenures to save on overall interest cost. But if you are not able to manage higher EMIs or have limited repayment capacity, you can opt for longer tenures and consider prepaying your loan when you have adequate funds, keeping aside emergency funds.

  • Personal Loan Balance Transfer to Reduce Interest Rates

  • Personal Loan Balance Transfer (PLBT) allows existing borrowers to transfer their outstanding loan balance from one lender to another at a lower interest rate, for a longer tenure and/or for better terms and conditions. The interest rate offered by the new lender would depend on the borrower’s credit score, income, existing outstanding loan amount, tenure and other aspects of their credit profile.



  • Borrowers should note that there are certain nominal charges, such as foreclosure charges and loan transfer fees payable to their existing lender. Additionally, the new lender may charge a processing fee, stamp duty and other fees that are associated with a fresh loan application. Therefore, it’s important to carefully evaluate whether the savings on interest from the balance transfer will outweigh these associated costs.

  • How MCLR Impacts Personal Loan Interest Rates?

  • ✓ The full form of MCLR is Marginal Cost of Funds-based Lending Rate
  • ✓ MCLR is the minimum interest rate below which banks cannot lend money
  • ✓ Each bank sets its own MCLR, depending on the cost of funds (like deposits) and RBI guidelines
  • ✓ Your personal loan interest rate is generally linked to the bank’s MCLR and if the bank modifies its MCLR, your EMI can change as well
  • ✓ A bank can review and revise its MCLR periodically depending on its banking policies.

  • Impact of RBI Regulations on Personal Loan Interest Rates

  • RBI monetary policy and its liquidity maneuvers can directly impact the cost of funding for the banks/NBFCs, which in turn will impact the interest rates for fresh borrowers. These will also impact existing borrowers who have availed loans at floating interest rates, subject to their interest rate reset date set by their lenders. However, these policy changes will not impact the interest rate of fixed rate personal loan borrowers as their interest rate will remain unchanged for their entire loan tenure.

    FAQs

    What is the current interest rate for personal loans? +
    Personal loan interest rates vary between banks and NBFCs, depending on factors such as your credit score, employment history, income and the lender’s credit risk policies. Currently, the personal loan interest rates offered by private sector banks start from 9.98% p.a. onwards. However, some public sector banks may offer personal loans at lower interest rates.
    Is GST application on personal loan interest? +
    GST is not applicable on the interest component of personal loans. However, a GST of 18% is levied on the various personal loan fees and charges levied by the lenders.
    What is a good interest rate on a personal loan? +
    There is no such thing as good personal loan interest rates. Lenders consider their cost of funds, net interest margins and the credit risk assessment of their loan applicants while setting their interest rates. Those with higher credit scores are likely to fetch lower interest rates on personal loan schemes. Therefore, loan applicants with higher credit scores should check and compare interest rates offered by multiple lenders to know the best offers available on their credit profiles.
    What are the minimum and maximum interest rates for a personal loan? +
    The interest rate for personal loans offered by private sector lenders usually starts from 9.99% p.a. Note that the lenders may offer lower personal loan interest rates than their published card rates, based on the borrower’s credit profile, subject to their floor rate for retail loans as per RBI’s guidelines.
    Is there any difference between salaried and self-employed personal loan rates? +
    Most lenders usually offer lower personal loan interest rates to their salaried applicants than self-employed applicants due to the higher income certainty of the former. Note that apart from the applicant’s occupation profile, lenders would also consider other factors of the loan applicant’s credit profile like his/her credit score, income profile, etc while setting his/her interest rates.
